Beginner Tips

1. Bend Your Knees

You must bend your knees when trying to balance and skate. The habit of bending your knees goes a long way.

2. Start on Smooth Ground

Practice on a flat and smooth surface. Try to identify if a surface is uneven as it can cause unintential falls.

3. Learn How to Fall Safely

Falling is part of learning. When do do feel like falling, FALL FORWARD. It is safer to fall forward rather than to fall back and hit your head. Again, FALL FORWARD.

4. Practice Stopping

Once you can skate, learn how to stop. There are several methods to stopping, so learn a few and practice what feels more natural. Do NOT learn stopping on a incline. It will be significantly harder.

5. Go at Your Own Pace

If you are around other skaters, there may be a pressure to be at their level fast. Take your time, remember they were once at your level. Rushing can lead to more accidents.

6. Have Fun

Roller skating is not only a skill, but it is also a social sport. Meet new people and learn from others.
